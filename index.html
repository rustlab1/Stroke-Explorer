Meta-analysis of 9 mouse MCAO scRNA-seq datasets — full transcriptome (~29 k gene symbols) across 12 cell types. Module scores are mean log-normalized expression of the genes in each panel; pathway modules are fully editable on the Cell Death tab.

Which cell-death pathway does each brain cell use after stroke — and when?
A cross-dataset transcriptomic atlas of ischemic stroke at single-cell resolution. Nine MCAO / photothrombosis scRNA/snRNA-seq studies (mouse, rat, human; 500k+ cells) are harmonized here to let you trace apoptosis, ferroptosis, necroptosis, necrosis and parthanatos signatures across astrocytes, microglia, neurons, endothelia, pericytes and more — both at acute (D1-D3) and chronic (D14-D28) timepoints.

Cell death pathway scores
Research question: Which of the five major programmed-death pathways — apoptosis, ferroptosis, necroptosis, necrosis / pyroptosis, and parthanatos — dominates in each brain cell population after ischemic stroke, and how does this change from acute (D1-D3) through sub-acute (D4-D14) to chronic (>D14) phases? Every score below is computed live from the editable gene panels — add or remove genes and the heatmap updates instantly.
