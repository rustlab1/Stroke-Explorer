Phase 4 — Proteomics integration

Tremblay et al. 2024 (Fluids Barriers CNS) [48] performed in vivo glycocapture of the BBB luminal surface proteome in rat and mouse, identifying 1,000+ glycoproteins on the luminal EC surface. This is the most direct evidence for surface accessibility of any protein. Cross-referencing our 9 Class A–C targets against this proteome would provide protein-level confirmation of luminal surface accessibility — stronger than transcriptomic inference alone.

Shi et al. 2025 (Bio-protocol) [67] describes a refined luminal cerebrovascular proteomics protocol. The underlying dataset (from the Wyss-Coray lab) should be checked for public availability.

6. Key Gaps in the Current Landscape

GapImpactBest available resourceWhat is missing
Human stroke EC transcriptomics Critical — all primary datasets are mouse GSE9391 (microarray, 14/78 targets only) No human in vivo stroke scRNA-seq or sorted EC bulk RNA-seq dataset exists publicly. GSE311086 is in vitro only.
Protein-level surface expression in stroke High — transcriptomics ≠ surface protein expression Tremblay 2024 (healthy BBB proteome) No stroke-specific BBB luminal proteome dataset. Healthy reference only.
Reperfusion vs. permanent occlusion Medium — reperfusion changes EC activation profile GSE265949 (tMCAO 30min+reperfusion) Most datasets use permanent or 1h occlusion. Reperfusion-specific EC surface target dynamics not well characterized.
Spatial localization of EC targets Medium — penumbra vs. core vs. perilesional Han 2024 Sci Transl Med (spatial+scRNA-seq) Han 2024 GEO accession not yet identified. GSE288162 (spatial, cerebral microbleeds) is not stroke-specific.
Chronic phase EC targets (>7 days) Medium — relevant for subacute neuroprotection GSE279666 (Day14), GSE283970 (Day30 NHP) Limited data on EC surface targets beyond Day7. Angiogenic remodeling phase poorly characterized.
Sex differences in EC activation Low-medium — most datasets use male mice only None identified No dataset stratifies EC surface target expression by sex. Clinically relevant given sex differences in stroke outcomes.
